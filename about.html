About Me

Hi, I am a first-year student at Syracuse University, pursuing a double major in Computer Science and Mathematics. My academic journey is shaped by my research in areas such as measure theory, combinatorial graph theory, and sequence analysis.

Currently, I am researching the characterization of rectifiable measures on Lipschitz graphs, developing algorithms to check Lipschitz conditions. I am also exploring the Anti-van der Waerden number for various graph types and studying lexicographically least square-free words with given prefixes. By bridging theoretical mathematics with computational techniques, I aim to address complex problems and contribute to the advancement of these fields.

This site showcases my academic projects, research endeavors, and professional aspirations. Feel free to reach out to me and connect with me on linkedin.
